## The Problem

Traditional GDP metrics classify digital screen time as idle leisure consumption. But when you scroll a social media feed, you are simultaneously producing a distinct economic output — behavioral data that serves as a critical input to AI training, targeted advertising, and algorithmic product development. This production is uncompensated and unrecognized by any national accounting system.

## Our Approach

The Entangled Time framework introduces a third time state — Simultaneous Production-Consumption — that replaces the classical work/leisure partition with a four-state model. The model derives existence, uniqueness, and welfare results using formal mathematical proofs, and generates quantitative predictions that can be empirically tested and falsified.

## Key Insights

- **13 trillion hours/year** of unpaid data labor — 4× the total formal global labor force
- **$1.3 trillion/year** conservative Dark GDP (directly observable revenue channels)
- **$10–15 trillion/year** adjusted estimate including unmeasured channels
- **Zero-wage equilibrium** is the unique profit-maximizing outcome, not a moral failure
- The window for policy intervention is **closing** — cognitive capital depreciates irreversibly
